Publisher

Albert Stampone

The inspiration for Albert to launch the Downtown family of magazines developed thanks to a childhood love of reading books and, of course, magazines. That progressed into a love of writing. Prior to launching his first magazine in North Haledon and Hawthorne in 2000, he worked as a writer and editor for several papers and magazines, where he interviewed entertainment personalities such as Seth Meyers, Michelle Trachtenberg, Dave Attell, Chris Elliott and Stan Lee. He’s often asked why and even how he came up with the idea of publishing a magazine. “I developed the concept, I knew what the magazine would look like, I believed people would like it, and I was still in my twenties, so I went for it!”

In the 15 years since the launch of the first issue, Downtown’s editions have continued to grow and gain popularity among both readers and advertisers. Today, with nearly 20 years of experience in publishing, Albert’s expertise in marketing, advertising and brand strategy is instrumental in helping Downtown advertisers successfully promote their products and services.

William Dawes Schulz is the 7th great grandson of William Dawes Jr., who rode with (and farther than) Paul Revere on April 18, 1775. He was then promptly forgotten after poet Henry Longfellow decided that “Revere” was easier to rhyme than “Dawes.” Bill is the former co-host of the Fox News Channel talk show, Red Eye w/Greg Gutfeld.

Senior Writer

Contributing Writer

Casey Colaneri

Currently, he’s the chef at The Ridge Steak & Sushi in Airmont, N.Y. Casey studied in Orvieto, Italy for nearly a year learning secrets to homemade pastas, fresh breads and sweet desserts. Now he shares his cooking secrets with you in his recipes of the month.
